# Global Power Plant EDA

## Overview
In this project, I performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Global Power Plant Database. The goal is to understand how energy is produced around the world and to explore the difference between renewable and non-renewable energy sources.

---

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle (World Resources Institute)
- Rows: ~35,000
- Columns: ~20

The dataset includes information about power plants such as location, energy type, capacity, and year of operation.

---

## Objective
- Explore global energy production
- Compare renewable and non-renewable energy
- Identify patterns and trends in the data

---

## Data Cleaning
- Removed duplicate rows
- Handled missing values (removed or filled when needed)
- Fixed data types for some columns

---

## Visualizations
I created several plots to better understand the data, including:
- Distribution of energy types
- Renewable vs non-renewable comparison
- Capacity distribution
- Top countries by number of plants
- Correlation between numerical features
- Capacity differences across energy types
- Power plants over time

---

## Key Insights
- Most power plants still rely on fossil fuels like coal and gas  
- Renewable energy (especially solar and wind) is growing  
- A small number of plants produce a large portion of energy
